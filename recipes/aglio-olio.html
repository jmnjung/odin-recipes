Aglio e Olio

Description

Aglio e Olio is a cherished Italian pasta dish known for its simplicity and vibrant flavors. The name, which translates to "garlic and oil," perfectly captures the essence of this classic recipe. Made with just spaghetti, garlic, extra virgin olive oil, red pepper flakes, and parsley, this dish showcases the beauty of uncomplicated yet delicious cooking. The garlic is sautéed in olive oil until golden and fragrant, imparting a deep, savory richness. A sprinkle of red pepper flakes adds a gentle heat, while fresh parsley provides a burst of color and freshness.

Typically served as a comforting weeknight meal or a quick dish, Aglio e Olio is both versatile and satisfying. It pairs beautifully with a side of crusty bread or a simple green salad. The dish's minimalist approach highlights the importance of high-quality ingredients and careful preparation, making it a favorite among home cooks and chefs alike. Whether enjoyed in its classic form or with added ingredients like sautéed vegetables or protein, Aglio e Olio offers a delicious and gratifying experience that celebrates the art of Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

This YouTube video covers the basic ingredients and steps to make a dish of Aglio e Olio.

Required Ingredients:

  • Seven ounces of spaghetti
  • Half a cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • Six cloves of garlic
  • A teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • Fresh parsley
  • Salt

Additional Ingredients:

  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Lemon zest

Steps

  1. Finely chop the garlic and parsley.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add ten grams of salt for every liter of water.
  3. Cook the pasta in the salted water for three minutes less than suggested.
  4. In a pan, heat extra virgin olive oil over medium heat.
  5. Add the garlic to the pan and add the red pepper flakes once it starts to sizzle.
  6. Right before the garlic turns golden brown, add some pasta water to the pan.
  7. Add the pasta to the pan and cook until al dente for the starch to thicken the sauce.
  8. Add the chopped parsley and drizzle some olive oil to emulsify the sauce.
